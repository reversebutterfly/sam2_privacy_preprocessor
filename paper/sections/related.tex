\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Adversarial attacks on video segmentation.}
UAP-SAM2~\citep{uap_sam2_2025} proposes universal adversarial perturbations against SAM2, achieving $-45.8$\,pp mIoU on DAVIS with feature-space objectives.
DarkSAM~\citep{darksam2024} attacks the SAM image predictor via prompt-agnostic perturbations.
Both operate under $L_\infty$-bounded additive perturbation budgets without access to content or masks.
Our work differs fundamentally: we study \emph{publisher-side structured editing} with full mask knowledge, targeting codec robustness rather than perturbation imperceptibility.
UAP-SAM2 does not evaluate under H.264 compression; our prior feature-space experiments confirm that FPN-shift attacks collapse to near-zero effectiveness ($\Delta\mathrm{JF}_\mathrm{codec} < 0.01$) under CRF=23.

\paragraph{Codec-robust adversarial perturbations.}
RoVISQ~\citep{rovisq2023} demonstrates codec-surviving attacks against video \emph{classifiers} by optimising in the DCT domain.
The key difference from our setting: classifiers aggregate temporal features, providing rich gradient signal from codec-surviving low-frequency components.
SAM2's per-frame memory write operates at an $8{\times}8$ spatial bottleneck, which may further filter perturbation energy.

\paragraph{Privacy-preserving video processing.}
Traditional approaches include face blurring~\citep{mcpherson2016defeating}, Fawkes~\citep{shan2020fawkes} (face recognition cloaking), and AdvCloak~\citep{advcloak} (identity perturbation).
These target face-specific recognition systems and do not address video object tracking, codec compression, or non-face objects.
Our method is complementary: it targets generic object tracking by exploiting boundary-level codec interactions rather than identity-level perturbations.

\paragraph{Adversarial purification.}
H.264 CRF=23 acts as an implicit adversarial purifier~\citep{yoon2021adv_purification,diffpure2022} via DCT quantization, destroying high-frequency adversarial patterns without any explicit defense design.
Our method deliberately creates \emph{low-frequency} structured edits that survive this purification, inverting the typical attacker-defender dynamic.
